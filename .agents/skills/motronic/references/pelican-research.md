## How To Use Forum Advice In Answers

- Summarize forum advice in your own words. Do not copy long posts.
- Cite the thread title or URL when it materially supports the answer.
- Label forum-derived material as practical field evidence, not factory specification.
- Prefer threads with a final repair outcome. If the thread is unresolved, say so.
- Convert anecdotes into tests: what to measure, what result means, and what branch follows.
- When forum advice conflicts with factory data, use the forum post as a hypothesis and resolve the conflict with measurement.

## High-Value Pelican-Derived Heuristics

- On 3.2 Motronic no-starts, first clarify `cranks` vs `no crank`; owners often say no-start for both.
- Do not condemn the fuel pump only because it is not heard with key on. Check DME relay outputs, cranking behavior, and fuel pressure.
- After engine removal or clutch work, speed/reference sensor connector order, air gap, and damaged insulation deserve early attention.
- A true software rev limiter is consistent. Load-sensitive breakup or tach mismatch points elsewhere.
- Verify tach accuracy before accepting a reported low rev limit.
- Before depinning any old harness connector, take photos, tag wires, and verify against a diagram or trusted pin map.
- For performance 3.2 builds, define the target before parts selection. Chip/exhaust gains are limited; larger changes require a matched engine, induction, exhaust, and tuning package.
- Do not rely on advertised compression or piston specs alone. Measure the engine parts in hand.
